The Point-to-Multipoint topology (also called star topology or simply P2MP) is a common network architecture for outdoor wireless networks to connect multiple locations to one single central location. In a point-to-multipoint wireless Ethernet network, all remote locations do not communicate directly with each other but have a single connection towards the centre of the star network where one or more base station is typically located.

Wireless Internet Service Provider (WISP) markets are one of the more prevalent uses of 99Rich Solutions products. Users have been increasingly demanding not only more bandwidth but also access to the Internet from more locations.

WISPs exist because some areas are tough to serve through traditional land lines or because the time to lay new land lines takes too long.

WISP companies face challenges of additional government regulatory control due to the RF component while also needing to face the increasing challenges of access distribution, control, and monitoring. Overwhelming consumer acceptance and adoption of wireless internet technologies has meant that WISPs must constantly evaluate their networks to ensure compatibility with new applications, efficiency of the RF resource, and expansion into new often remote markets.
